Glioma Tumors

About Glioma

A glioma is a type of tumor that forms when glial cells grow uncontrollably. Gliomas can develop in the brain or spinal cord, though they are most commonly found in the brain. These tumors are usually malignant, though their growth rate can vary from slow to aggressive.

Meningioma Tumors

About Meningioma

A meningioma is a tumor that grows from the meninges, the membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord. While not a brain tumor, it may press on the brain or nerves. Meningiomas grow slowly, but in some cases, they can cause serious disability by affecting nearby tissues.

Pituitary Tumors

About Pituitary Tumors

Pituitary tumors are unusual growths in the pituitary gland, a pea-sized organ behind the nose. These tumors may cause the gland to produce too much or too little of certain hormones, leading to various health issues. While mostly benign, their location in sensitive areas can cause serious health concerns.
